Introduction to Plain Language

Plain language is a style of writing that prioritizes clarity and simplicity, making information easy to understand for a broad audience. It involves using straightforward vocabulary, concise sentences, and a logical structure to ensure that the intended message is communicated effectively without unnecessary complexity.

Why does plain language matter?

  • Accessibility: Plain language makes information accessible to everyone, including those with varying levels of literacy, non-native speakers, and individuals with cognitive disabilities.
  • Efficiency: Clear communication reduces misunderstandings and the need for follow-up questions, saving time for both the writer and the reader.
  • Engagement: When information is easy to understand, readers are more likely to stay engaged and retain the information.
  • Trust: Transparent and straightforward communication builds trust between the writer and the audience, whether in business, government, or everyday interactions.

By adopting plain language principles, writers can ensure their messages are inclusive, effective, and impactful.

Plain Language Summary

The plain language guidelines aim to make written communication more accessible and understandable. They emphasize knowing your audience and tailoring content to their needs. Key strategies include organizing text to highlight main points first, using headings and lists to break up dense information, and avoiding technical jargon and complex sentences. Active voice is preferred for clarity, and consistency in style and terminology is crucial. While these guidelines are essential for clear communication, occasional deviations may occur as writers address the complexities of language.

Verbs Explained

Original paragraph

The environmental impacts of urbanization have been significant, particularly concerning the increase in air pollution levels and the reduction in green spaces due to the expansion of infrastructure. Cities have been experiencing a rise in air pollutants resulting from increased vehicular emissions and industrial activities. This has been exacerbated by the reduction in natural environments, which has decreased the capacity for city landscapes to filter out pollution naturally. Noise pollution has also become predominant, with the increasing population density leading to a surge in transportation noise and general urban activity. These environmental challenges have prompted cities to adopt various measures aimed at mitigating their impacts, such as the implementation of green roofs and the creation of urban parks. Moreover, public transportation systems have been expanded and improved to reduce reliance on personal vehicles. Efforts to promote sustainable practices are continuing, as are initiatives to enhance urban resilience in the face of environmental challenges.

Revised paragraph

Urbanization has significant environmental impact, particularly with the increase in air pollution levels and the reduction of green spaces due to infrastructure expansion. Cities have experienced a rise in air pollutants from increased vehicular emissions and industrial activities. This issue is exacerbated by the reduction in natural environments, decreasing the capacity of city landscapes to filter out pollution naturally. Additionally, cities expanded and improved public transportation systems to reduce reliance on personal vehicles. Efforts to promote sustainable practices continue, along with initiatives to enhance urban resilience in the face of environmental challenges.
